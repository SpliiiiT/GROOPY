The story

EfficientNetB0 won the weighted scorecard (accuracy + robustness), but it's the slowest (~297 ms/frame). cnn_scratch is the real-time, deployable model (~26 ms, 15 MB, 99.3%). Re-weight toward latency and the baseline wins — the scorecard encodes priorities.

Track A · Word · Explainability

Word XAI — gradient saliency (the Grad-CAM analog)

The word model has no images, so instead of Grad-CAM we use input-gradient saliency: the gradient of the predicted class w.r.t. the input landmarks tells us which frames and which body parts drove the decision.

Where the model looks — over time and over the body

The trust check

Just like Grad-CAM proves the CNN looks at the hand, this proves the word model relies on the hands and the motion — not on incidental body position. That's exactly what the landmark-normalization step was designed to enforce.

Sentiment · Explainability

Sentiment XAI — the model's word weights

The from-scratch model is linear (TF-IDF + Logistic Regression), so it's intrinsically interpretable — stronger than a post-hoc heatmap. We can read the exact coefficient (log-odds) each word contributes, straight from the trained model.

Top words pushing → positive

Top words pushing → negative

Why this matters

The weights are human-readable and sensible (great, excellent, perfect vs bad, worst, awful) — you can audit exactly why any prediction was made. No black box, and no approximation needed to explain it.

Track B · Synthesis

Text/Speech → Sign

Track B is a deterministic, rule-based pipeline, not a trained model — so it has no training curves or metrics by design. Here's what it produces instead.

The pipeline

speech / text→text_to_gloss→ build_sign_plan→apply_sentiment→player

Worked example

input:  "Hi, thank you Oussama"
glosses: [hello, thanks, oussama]  (hi→hello, thank you→thanks)
plan:    hello · thanks · [O-U-S-S-A-M-A]
render:  play hello.mp4 → play thanks.mp4 → fingerspell 7 letters

Known words play their sign clip; out-of-vocabulary words (like a name) fall back to fingerspelling, so the system never dead-ends.

If asked "where are Track B's metrics?"

It's evaluated by correctness of the plan, not accuracy — the same input always yields the same signs. Its quality bar is coverage (every word maps to a clip or letters) and the headless plan-builder is covered by the smoke tests.
